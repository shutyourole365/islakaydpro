Title: Just launched my SaaS after 6 months - here's what I learned

After 6 months of building, I launched Islakayd today (equipment rental marketplace).

Here are the lessons I wish I knew on day 1:

**1. Start with ONE problem**
I wanted to build 50 features. Started with 3. Still took 2 months. Features can wait. Solve the core problem first.

**2. Talk to users BEFORE building**
I interviewed 20 potential users. Changed my entire pricing model based on feedback. Saved months of wasted development.

**3. Launch earlier than comfortable**
I waited too long to launch. Should have launched at 70% done. Getting real user feedback beats perfecting in isolation.

**4. Technical debt is real**
Cutting corners early meant 3 weeks of refactoring later. Write clean code from the start, even if it's slower.

**5. Marketing = 50% of the work**
Built an amazing product. Had zero users. Realized building is only half the battle. Marketing is equally important.

**6. Focus on ONE channel**
Tried Twitter, Instagram, Reddit, Product Hunt, LinkedIn, TikTok all at once. Spread too thin. Now focusing on Product Hunt + Reddit only.

**7. Automation saves sanity**
Automated: deployments, testing, backups. Manual: customer support (for now). Time spent on automation paid off 10x.

**8. Pricing is hard**
Changed pricing 4 times. Still not sure it's right. Going to let the market decide.

**9. Community > followers**
100 engaged Reddit users > 10K silent Twitter followers. Build community, not just audience.

Perfectionists don't ship. Pragmatists do. Ship, iterate, improve.
